What others said about Nitrate Online

A cinemag founded by Seattle journalist Carrie Gorringe, NO contains thoughtful film critiques, longer features (a tribute to Roger Corman, another look at the rereleased A Clockwork Orange), interviews (Errol Morris, Roman Polanski), and news-wire dispatches. The design may be minimal, but Gorringe and her crew patently adore movies, which is why other people who adore movies will enjoy their work.

B+ — Entertainment Weekly, 14 April 2000

A new local site for movie-lovers … they review current releases and run features on film classics. Good writing outweighs the lack of visual pizazz. Keep an eye on these guys, though.

— KMTT, Seattle

The newest word in film reportage, Nitrate Online equips you with hefty feature-length articles and reviews that tell more than just the story line. The Seattle-based entertainment magazine plans to review books in the future.

— NetGuide Live

This straight-forward site is for movie lovers who take film seriously; look for reviews that are substantial and intelligent.

— CYphon — Lifestyle Resources for the Film and Video Professional
