Feature Essay  ·  1969

The Wild Bunch

Directed by Sam Peckinpah  ·  by Gregory Avery

Peckinpah's elegy to the American West stands as one of the most viscerally honest westerns ever committed to film. Shot with a ruthless precision that matches its subject, the film dissects the mythology of the frontier and finds only mortality beneath it.

About

Nitrate Online was an independent film criticism publication, named for the nitrate film stock of cinema's golden age. It published reviews, essays, and festival dispatches from 1996 to 2004.
